Chapter 3 – Keyframing

Using keyframes with effects and clips in Adobe After Effects is fundamental to creating dynamic animations, transitions, and visual effects. Here's a brief explanation of how to utilize keyframes effectively:

Understanding Keyframes:
Keyframes are markers on the timeline that denote specific points in time where you define a parameter's value. These values can include properties of clips (e.g., position, scale, opacity) and effects (e.g., blur, color adjustments).
By setting keyframes at different times with varying values, you create smooth transitions and animations.
Setting Keyframes for Clips:
To animate clips, select a property (e.g., position) in the timeline, and click the stopwatch icon next to it. This action sets the initial keyframe with the current value. Move the playhead to a different time, adjust the property value, and click the stopwatch again to set another keyframe.
As the playhead moves between keyframes, After Effects interpolates the values, creating a smooth animation.
Using Keyframes for Effects:
Apply an effect to a layer and open the effect's properties. Similar to clips, select a parameter you want to animate (e.g., blur amount), click the stopwatch icon, and set an initial keyframe.
Move the playhead, modify the parameter value, and click the stopwatch to add another keyframe. Adjusting the property over time between keyframes will animate the effect.
Manipulating Keyframes:
After placing keyframes, you can move them in the timeline to change the timing of the animation. You can also adjust their values to modify the animation's behavior.
Right-click on a keyframe to access options like easing (smooth transitions), easing out (gradually slowing down), and easing in (gradually speeding up).
Interpolation and Bezier Handles:
After Effects offers Bezier handles that allow you to fine-tune the interpolation between keyframes. Adjusting these handles enables you to control the speed and smoothness of the animation, allowing for more natural and dynamic movements.
By skillfully utilizing keyframes with effects and clips, you can bring your compositions to life with intricate animations, seamless transitions, and captivating visual effects in Adobe After Effects.
